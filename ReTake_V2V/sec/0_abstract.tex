\begin{abstract}
Precise camera control for video reshooting remains a significant challenge for video models, primarily due to a lack of large-scale, paired multi-view video data for training. We overcome this data bottleneck by introducing a novel self-supervised method to train robust camera control models using only widely available monocular videos. Our core contribution is a highly scalable strategy to generate a "pseudo multi-view training triplet" (a source, anchor, and target video) from a single video. We first extract two distinct, "smooth random walk trajectories" to serve as the source and target clips. We then synthetically generate the anchor video by warping the source's first frame with a dense tracking field, simulating the distorted, point-cloud-based input used at inference. This setup forces the model to implicitly learn the underlying 3D scene structure by sourcing and re-projecting content across distinct viewpoints and times from the source video to reconstruct the target video, guided by the anchor video. By training on this domain-agnostic and massively scalable data with minimal architectural changes, our model enables high-quality, consistent camera control across a diverse set of videos, substantially advancing the capabilities of video reshooting.
\end{abstract}
